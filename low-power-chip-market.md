<p><p>The low power chip market encompasses various power consumption types essential for efficient device performance. Dynamic power consumption refers to energy used during active operation, typically fluctuating with workload changes. Static power consumption, on the other hand, occurs when devices are idle, representing a constant drain on energy. Surge power pertains to brief spikes in energy demand during high-performance tasks. Together, these categories influence the design and implementation of chips in portable and energy-sensitive applications, driving innovation in low power technologies.</p></p>
