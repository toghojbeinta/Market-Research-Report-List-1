<p><p>The fibrin medical adhesive market comprises several types, each with distinct characteristics. Water-based adhesives are derived from natural sources and offer biocompatibility, suitable for soft tissue applications. Solvent-based adhesives contain solvents that evaporate, creating a strong bond but may have regulatory limitations due to toxicity. Solid adhesives come in the form of strips or powders that require activation for use, while hot melt adhesives are thermoplastics that bond upon cooling, providing quick and effective adhesion in surgical settings.</p></p>
